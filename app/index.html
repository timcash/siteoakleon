Oakleon

Software to build on

End to end field service software

Customer management

Store and full text search all of your customer details with flexible record formats.

Scheduling

Set up recuring appointments and have SMS or emails automatically send for confirmmation.

Routing

Automatically generated routes to reduce fuel cost and time spent driving. Routes adapt to new scheduling information.

Sales

Let your sales team operate using mobile devices and see how thier teamates are performing.

Analytics

Create a living dashboard that is always up to date with the information you need to keep your buisness running. All data is easily exportable in a number of popular formats.

Auditing

Every change to the system is recorded and made available to users you give permissions to. Rollback to previous versions of a record at the click of a button.

Payments

Take reccuring credit card payments and see an account balance for each location. Let customers pay via thier own page and view thier history.

Fleet GPS

See your fleet in realtime with the ability to set geofences and speed limits. Get alerts based on the rules you define.

Jason Walton Co-founder

Mr. Walton leads the business development and marketing for Oakleon. Prior to co-founding Oakleon with Mr. Cash, he worked in the pest control industry. Since 1997, Mr. Walton founded 15 pest control companies with 22 branch locations in 8 different states. Mr. Walton's brands have included Moxie Pest Control, Homefront Pest Control, Joshua's Pest Control, and Mayday Pest Control. The companies founded and operated by Mr. Walton have combined value over $60 million in annual revenue. Prior to pest control ownership, Mr. Walton worked with Eclipse Marketing. He received a B.A. in Accounting from Brigham Young University.

Tim Cash Co-founder

Tim is leading the engineering effort at Oakleon. Before Oakleon he spent six years in the Air Force and ten years in the Intelligence Community where he led analytic and development teams. Tim holds a degree in Applied Mathematics and (most) of another in Computer Science. Much of his time is spent thinking about better ways to distribute and make decisions based upon complex and near realtime information.
